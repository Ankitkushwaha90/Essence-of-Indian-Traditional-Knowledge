# Indian Constitutional Law - Exam Preparation Notes

## 1. Meaning of Constitutional Law and Constitutionalism

**Constitutional Law**: The body of law that defines the roles, powers, and structure of the executive, legislature, and judiciary.

**Constitutionalism**: The idea that government power is limited by law; it promotes the **rule of law**, **separation of powers**, and **protection of fundamental rights**.

---

## 2. Historical Background of the Constituent Assembly

- Recommended by the **Cabinet Mission Plan (1946)**.
- Constituted in **December 1946**, chaired by **Dr. Rajendra Prasad**.
- **Drafting Committee** chaired by **Dr. B.R. Ambedkar**.
- Had **299 members** representing provinces, princely states, and communities.
- Draft completed in **1949**; adopted on **26 November 1949**, enforced on **26 January 1950**.

---

## 3. Government of India Act, 1935

Most comprehensive British legislation for India.

### Key Features:
- Proposed **All-India Federation** (never implemented).
- Introduced **provincial autonomy**.
- **Bicameral central legislature**.
- Established a **Federal Court**.
- Gave **residuary powers** to the Governor-General.

---

## 4. Indian Independence Act, 1947

- Passed by British Parliament.
- Declared **independence of India and Pakistan**.
- Ended British rule; created two dominions.
- Granted power to **Constituent Assemblies**.
- Abolished **Secretary of State** office.

---

## 5. Enforcement of the Constitution

- **Adopted**: 26 November 1949  
- **Enforced**: 26 January 1950 (Republic Day)  
- Immediate enforcement: **Citizenship, Elections, Provisional Parliament, Temporary provisions**

---

## 6. Indian Constitution and Its Salient Features

- **Lengthiest written constitution**.
- **Borrowed features** from UK, US, Canada, etc.
- **Federal system** with a **unitary bias**.
- **Parliamentary system**.
- **Independent judiciary**.
- **Fundamental Rights and Duties**.
- **Directive Principles of State Policy (DPSPs)**.
- **Emergency provisions**.
- **Single citizenship**.

---

## 7. The Preamble of the Constitution

- **Introduction** to the Constitution.
- Declares India as: **Sovereign, Socialist, Secular, Democratic Republic**.
- Ensures: **Justice, Liberty, Equality, Fraternity**.
- Not enforceable in court, but guides constitutional interpretation.

---

## 8. Fundamental Rights (Articles 12–35)

- **Right to Equality** (14–18)  
- **Right to Freedom** (19–22)  
- **Right against Exploitation** (23–24)  
- **Right to Freedom of Religion** (25–28)  
- **Cultural and Educational Rights** (29–30)  
- **Right to Constitutional Remedies** (32)

---

## 9. Fundamental Duties (Article 51A)

- Added by **42nd Amendment (1976)**.
- Inspired by **USSR Constitution**.
- 11 duties including:
  - Respect the Constitution and national flag.
  - Promote harmony.
  - Protect environment.

---

## 10. Directive Principles of State Policy (DPSPs) (Articles 36–51)

- Guidelines for governance.
- Not enforceable by courts.
- Aims for **social and economic democracy**.

### Types:
- **Socialist** (e.g., Article 39)
- **Gandhian** (e.g., Article 40)
- **Liberal** (e.g., Article 44)

---

## 11. Parliamentary System

- Based on **UK model**.
- **Executive accountable to Legislature**.
- **President** – nominal head; **Prime Minister** – real executive.
- **Bicameral Parliament**: Lok Sabha & Rajya Sabha.

---

## 12. Federal System

- Dual polity, division of powers.
- Supremacy of the Constitution.
- Independent judiciary.

### Power Distribution:
- **Union List**
- **State List**
- **Concurrent List** (7th Schedule)

> Note: Integrated judiciary and All-India Services show **unitary features**.

---

## 13. Centre-State Relations

### a) Legislative (Art. 245–255)
- Union, State, Concurrent Lists.

### b) Administrative (Art. 256–263)
- Centre's direction, Inter-State Council.

### c) Financial (Art. 268–293)
- **Finance Commission**
- **Grants-in-aid**

---

## 14. Amendment of the Constitution (Article 368)

### Types of Amendments:
- By **simple majority**
- By **special majority**
- By **special majority + state ratification**

> **Basic Structure Doctrine** (Kesavananda Bharati Case, 1973):  
> Parliament cannot alter the **basic structure**.

---

## 15. Historical Perspective of Constitutional Amendments

- Over **100 amendments**.
  
### Key Amendments:
- **1st** – Restrictions on free speech.
- **42nd** – “Mini Constitution” (centralized power).
- **44th** – Removed Emergency misuse (1978).
- **73rd/74th** – Local Self-Government (1992).
- **86th** – Right to Education.
- **103rd** – EWS Reservation (2019).

---

## 16. Emergency Provisions

### a) National Emergency (Article 352)
- Due to war, external aggression, armed rebellion.
- Suspends most Fundamental Rights (except 20 & 21).

### b) President’s Rule (Article 356)
- Failure of constitutional machinery in states.

### c) Financial Emergency (Article 360)
- Threat to financial stability.
- Centre controls state finances.

---

## 17. Local Self-Government – Constitutional Scheme

### 73rd Amendment (1992): **Panchayati Raj Institutions**
- 3-tier: **Gram Panchayat → Panchayat Samiti → Zila Parishad**
- Articles **243–243O**

### 74th Amendment (1992): **Urban Local Bodies**
- **Nagar Panchayat**, **Municipal Council**, **Municipal Corporation**
- Articles **243P–243ZG**
