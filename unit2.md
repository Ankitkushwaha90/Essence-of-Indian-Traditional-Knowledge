# 📚 Indian Constitutional Law – Exam Preparation Notes

---

## 1. Meaning of Constitutional Law and Constitutionalism

**Constitutional Law**: The body of law which defines the role, powers, and structure of different entities within a state – the executive, the legislature, and the judiciary.

**Constitutionalism**: The idea that the government's power is limited by law. It promotes:
- Rule of law
- Separation of powers
- Protection of fundamental rights

---

## 2. Historical Background of the Constituent Assembly

- Recommended by Cabinet Mission Plan (1946)
- Constituted in December 1946, chaired by **Dr. Rajendra Prasad**
- **Drafting Committee** chaired by **Dr. B.R. Ambedkar**
- 299 members representing provinces, princely states, and communities
- Draft Constitution completed in 1949
- Adopted on 26 November 1949, enforced on 26 January 1950

---

## 3. Government of India Act, 1935

Most comprehensive British legislation for India.

### Features:
- Proposed All-India Federation (not implemented)
- Provincial autonomy
- Bicameral central legislature
- Federal court established
- Residuary powers to Governor-General

---

## 4. Indian Independence Act, 1947

- Passed by British Parliament
- Declared independence of India and Pakistan
- Ended British rule and created two dominions
- Granted power to Constituent Assemblies
- Abolished the office of Secretary of State

---

## 5. Enforcement of the Constitution

- **Adopted**: 26 November 1949  
- **Enforced**: 26 January 1950 (Republic Day)

### Provisions Enforced Immediately:
- Citizenship
- Elections
- Provisional Parliament
- Temporary provisions

---

## 6. Indian Constitution and Its Salient Features

- Lengthiest written constitution
- Drawn from various sources (UK, US, Canada, etc.)
- Federal system with a unitary bias
- Parliamentary system
- Independent judiciary
- Fundamental Rights & Duties
- Directive Principles of State Policy (DPSPs)
- Emergency provisions
- Single citizenship

---

## 7. The Preamble of the Constitution

- **Introduction** to the Constitution
- Declares India as:  
  **Sovereign**, **Socialist**, **Secular**, **Democratic Republic**
- Ensures:  
  **Justice**, **Liberty**, **Equality**, **Fraternity**
- Preamble is **not enforceable**, but serves as a guide to interpretation

---

## 8. Fundamental Rights (Articles 12–35)

- **Right to Equality** (14–18)
- **Right to Freedom** (19–22)
- **Right against Exploitation** (23–24)
- **Right to Freedom of Religion** (25–28)
- **Cultural and Educational Rights** (29–30)
- **Right to Constitutional Remedies** (32)

---

## 9. Fundamental Duties (Article 51A)

- Added by **42nd Amendment (1976)**
- Inspired by USSR Constitution
- 11 Duties including:
  - Respect the Constitution and national symbols
  - Promote harmony
  - Protect the environment
  - Safeguard public property

---

## 10. Directive Principles of State Policy (Articles 36–51)

- Guidelines for the state in governance
- **Not enforceable** by courts
- Aim: Establish **social and economic democracy**

### Types:
- **Socialist Principles** (e.g., Article 39)
- **Gandhian Principles** (e.g., Article 40)
- **Liberal-Intellectual Principles** (e.g., Article 44)

---

## 11. Parliamentary System

- Based on **UK model**
- Executive is **accountable to legislature**
- **President**: Nominal head  
- **Prime Minister**: Real executive
- Bicameral Parliament:
  - **Lok Sabha**
  - **Rajya Sabha**

---

## 12. Federal System

Features:
- Dual polity
- Division of powers
- Supremacy of the Constitution
- Independent judiciary

### Lists (7th Schedule):
- Union List
- State List
- Concurrent List

> ⚠️ Integrated judiciary & All-India Services reflect **unitary bias**

---

## 13. Centre-State Relations

### a) Legislative Relations (Articles 245–255)
- Division: Union List, State List, Concurrent List

### b) Administrative Relations (Articles 256–263)
- Centre can give directions
- Establishment of Inter-State Council

### c) Financial Relations (Articles 268–293)
- Finance Commission
- Grants-in-aid
- Tax sharing

---

## 14. Amendment of the Constitution (Article 368)

### Types of Amendment:
- **Simple majority**
- **Special majority**
- **Special majority + state ratification**

> ✅ **Kesavananda Bharati Case (1973)**:  
> Established the **Basic Structure Doctrine** – Parliament cannot alter the basic structure of the Constitution

---

## 15. Historical Perspective of Constitutional Amendments

India has passed over **100 amendments**.

### Major Amendments:
- **1st (1951)**: Restricted Right to Freedom of Speech
- **42nd (1976)**: “Mini-Constitution”, increased central power
- **44th (1978)**: Reversed Emergency powers
- **73rd & 74th (1992)**: Local self-government
- **86th (2002)**: Right to education
- **103rd (2019)**: 10% EWS reservation

---

## 16. Emergency Provisions

### a) National Emergency (Article 352)
- Conditions: War, External aggression, Armed rebellion
- **Effects**: Suspension of Fundamental Rights (except Articles 20 & 21), Centralized power

### b) President’s Rule (Article 356)
- Triggered by failure of constitutional machinery in a state
- Parliament assumes state legislative powers

### c) Financial Emergency (Article 360)
- Threat to financial stability
- Centre can control state expenditures and salaries

---

## 17. Local Self Government – Constitutional Scheme

### 73rd Amendment Act (1992) – Panchayati Raj

- Applies to **rural areas**
- 3-tier structure:
  - Gram Panchayat
  - Panchayat Samiti
  - Zila Parishad
- Covered under **Articles 243–243O**

### 74th Amendment Act (1992) – Urban Local Bodies

- Applies to **urban areas**
- 3 types:
  - Nagar Panchayat (transitional)
  - Municipal Council (small towns)
  - Municipal Corporation (large cities)
- Covered under **Articles 243P–243ZG**
