## ✅ 1. The Legal System: Sources of Law

### A. Enacted Law (Statute Law)
- Made by **Parliament or legislature**
- Known as **primary legislation** (Acts of Parliament)
- Binding and enforceable across the country

**Examples**:
- Indian Penal Code (IPC)
- Contract Act
- Constitution of India

### B. Common Law / Case Law
- Derived from **judicial decisions**
- Also known as **judge-made law**
- Based on the principle of **precedent** (stare decisis)
- Binding on lower courts if laid down by higher courts
- Important for **interpreting statutes** and **filling legal gaps**

## ✅ 2. Court System in India and Foreign Countries

### A. India

| Level         | Name              | Functions                                                                 |
|---------------|-------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Top           | Supreme Court     | Apex court, constitutional, appellate, original jurisdiction (Art. 32, 131, 136) |
| State         | High Courts       | Highest court in the state, hears civil, criminal, constitutional, and writ petitions |
| District      | District Courts   | Civil and criminal jurisdiction, headed by District & Sessions Judges     |
| Quasi-judicial| Forums & Tribunals| Consumer Forums, NCLT, CAT – handle specific matters like consumer rights, company law, tax |

### B. Foreign Examples

| Country | Key Courts                                                                 |
|---------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| UK      | Magistrates' Courts → Crown Court → Court of Appeal → Supreme Court of the UK |
| USA     | District Courts → Courts of Appeals → Supreme Court of the United States     |

## ✅ 3. Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR) – Arbitration

**Definition**:  
A private, binding process where disputes are resolved by an **arbitrator** instead of a judge.

### Key Points:
- **Voluntary**: Parties agree in a contract to resolve disputes through arbitration.
- **Binding Decision**: Award by the arbitrator is enforceable like a court decree.

### ✅ Advantages:
- **Faster and more cost-effective** than litigation
- Maintains **privacy and confidentiality**
- **Reduces burden** on courts

## ✅ 4. Branches of Law

### A. Contract Law
- Governs agreements enforceable by law (Indian Contract Act, 1872)

#### Key Elements:
- Offer and acceptance
- Legal consideration
- Intention to create legal relations
- Capacity to contract

**Breach leads to remedies**:  
- Damages  
- Specific performance  
- Injunction

---

### B. Tort Law
- Deals with **civil wrongs** causing harm or loss

#### Examples:
- Negligence
- Defamation
- Nuisance

**Remedies**:
- Compensation
- Injunction

> ⚠️ No need for a contract between parties

---

### C. Law at Workplace (Labour Law / Employment Law)
- Governs the relationship between **employers and employees**

#### Covers:
- Employment contracts
- Minimum wages
- Grievance redressal
- Industrial disputes
- Workplace safety (Factories Act, Industrial Disputes Act, etc.)

✅ Ensures fair treatment and dispute resolution
