# 📘 Intellectual Property Laws & IT Regulations – Exam Notes

---

## ✅ 1. Intellectual Property Laws: Introduction

- IP Law protects **creations of the mind**: innovations, literary/artistic works, symbols, names, images.
- **Types**: Patents, Copyrights, Trademarks, Designs, Trade Secrets.
- Ensures **economic rights** and **legal protection** for creators and innovators.

---

## ✅ 2. Legal Aspects of Patents

- **Patent**: Legal protection for inventions that are novel, non-obvious, and industrially applicable.
- Governed by the **Indian Patents Act, 1970**.
- **Term**: 20 years from the filing date.
- Must disclose the invention **fully**.

---

## ✅ 3. Filing of Patent Applications

**Process**:
1. Provisional/Complete specification.
2. Filing with the Indian Patent Office (IPO).
3. Request for Examination (RFE).
4. Publication after 18 months.
5. Examination and clearance of objections.
6. Grant of patent.

> 📌 Can be filed **online or physically**.

---

## ✅ 4. Rights from Patents

- Exclusive rights to **make, use, sell, or license** the invention.
- Right to **prevent others** from using the invention without permission.
- Monetary benefits from **licensing** or **commercialization**.

---

## ✅ 5. Infringement of Patents

**Unauthorized use** of patented invention.

Legal actions include:
- **Injunctions**
- **Damages** or account of profits
- **Seizure/destruction** of infringing goods

---

## ✅ 6. Copyright and Its Ownership

- Protects **original literary, artistic, musical, and dramatic works** (including software).
- **Automatic protection** from creation (registration is advisable but not required).
- **Term**: Lifetime of the author + 60 years.
- Owner has **economic** and **moral rights**.

---

## ✅ 7. Infringement of Copyright

Occurs when someone uses the work without permission:
- **Copying**, distributing, or broadcasting.
- Applies to both **physical** and **digital** formats.

---

## ✅ 8. Civil Remedies for Infringement

- **Injunctions** (temporary/permanent)
- **Damages** or profits earned through infringement
- **Delivery up/destruction** of infringing goods
- **Account of profits** and legal costs

---

## ✅ 9. Regulation of Information – Introduction

- Need to regulate access, use, and dissemination of **electronic data**
- Legal framework for **transparency** and **cybersecurity**

**Laws**:
- **RTI Act, 2005**
- **IT Act, 2000**

---

## ✅ 10. Right to Information (RTI) Act, 2005

- Empowers citizens to **seek information** from public authorities
- Ensures **transparency** and **accountability** in governance
- Applies to all public bodies (except some intelligence/security agencies)
- Citizens can **appeal** in case of refusal

---

## ✅ 11. Information Technology (IT) Act, 2000

- Provides **legal recognition** to electronic records and e-commerce
- Defines **cybercrimes** and legal mechanisms to address them
- Recognizes **digital signatures** and **electronic governance**

---

## ✅ 12. Electronic Governance (E-Governance)

Legal recognition of:
- **Electronic filing** of documents
- **Electronic communication** between citizens and the government

> Encourages **paperless governance**

---

## ✅ 13. Secure Electronic Records & Digital Signatures

- Ensures **authenticity** and **integrity** of digital records
- **Digital signature** = cryptographic authentication tool
- Secure records can’t be altered **without detection**

---

## ✅ 14. Digital Signature Certificates (DSC)

- Issued by **Certifying Authorities (CA)**

Ensures:
- **Identity** of the signer
- **Integrity** of the document

> Required for e-filing (e.g., **MCA**, **GST**, **ITR**)

---

## ✅ 15. Cyber Regulations Appellate Tribunal (CRAT)

- Established under IT Act to **hear appeals** against orders by Adjudicating Officers
- Powers similar to a **civil court**
- Now replaced by **TDSAT** (Telecom Disputes Settlement and Appellate Tribunal)

---

## ✅ 16. Offences under IT Act

**Cybercrimes** include:
- Hacking
- Identity theft
- Phishing
- Cyberstalking
- Publishing obscene content
- Unauthorized access/damage to computer networks

**Punishments**:
- **Fines**
- **Imprisonment** (up to 3–10 years depending on severity)

---

## ✅ 17. Limitations of the Information Technology Act

- Limited coverage of newer threats (e.g., **AI-driven frauds**, **ransomware**)
- **Lack of awareness** and digital literacy among enforcement agencies
- Inadequate **privacy and data protection**
- **Cross-border jurisdiction** issues
