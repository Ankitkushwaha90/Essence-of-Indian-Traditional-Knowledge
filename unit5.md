## ✅ 1. Types of Business Organizations

### A. Sole Traders
- A single individual **owns**, **manages**, and **controls** the business.
- **Advantages**:
  - Easy setup
  - Full control
  - Direct profits
- **Disadvantages**:
  - Unlimited liability
  - Limited capital

### B. Partnerships
- Business owned by **two or more persons** (max. 50 as per Indian Partnership Act, 1932).
- **Partnership Deed** outlines rights, duties, and profit-sharing.

#### Types:
1. **General Partnership**
2. **Limited Partnership (LLP)**

- **Advantages**:
  - Shared responsibility
  - More capital
- **Disadvantages**:
  - Unlimited liability (except LLP)
  - Conflicts

## ✅ 2. Companies and the Companies Act

### A. Introduction
- Governed by the **Companies Act, 2013**.
- A company is a **separate legal entity** with **perpetual succession**.

### B. Formation of a Company
- **Incorporation**: Apply through **MCA portal** (SPICe+ form).
  
**Documents required**:
- Memorandum of Association (MOA)
- Articles of Association (AOA)
- PAN, TAN, DINs of directors

### C. Memorandum of Association (MOA)
- The **charter document** of the company.
- Contains:
  - **Name Clause**
  - **Registered Office Clause**
  - **Object Clause**
  - **Liability Clause**
  - **Capital Clause**

### D. Articles of Association (AOA)
- Contains **rules and regulations** for internal management.
- Subordinate to **MOA**.

### E. Prospectus
- A formal legal document inviting the public to buy shares/debentures.
- Must contain:
  - **Financial details**
  - **Business model**
  - **Risk factors**

### F. Shares
- Ownership units of a company.
  
**Types**:
- **Equity Shares** (voting rights)
- **Preference Shares** (priority for dividend)
  
> Traded on **stock exchanges**.

### G. Directors
- Elected individuals responsible for managing the company.
- Minimum:
  - 2 for **Private Ltd.**
  - 3 for **Public Ltd.**
- Appointed via **board meetings** or **shareholders**.

### H. General Meetings and Proceedings
- **Annual General Meeting (AGM)**: Mandatory for public companies.
- **Extraordinary General Meeting (EGM)**: For urgent matters.
- Decisions made through **resolutions** (ordinary/special).

### I. Auditor
- Appointed under **Sec 139 of the Companies Act**.
- Role: Examine **financial statements**, give **opinion on fairness**.
- Must be **independent** and **qualified** (CA).

### J. Winding Up
- **Legal procedure** to close down a company.
  
**Types**:
- **Voluntary Winding Up**
- **Compulsory Winding Up by tribunal/NCLT**

> Creditors are paid, and **assets are liquidated**.

## ✅ 3. E-Governance and Role of Engineers

### A. E-Governance
- Use of **ICT** (Information & Communication Technology) to deliver government services efficiently.
  
**Examples**:
- **Digital India**
- **e-Courts**
- **e-Seva**

### B. Role of Engineers
- Develop and maintain **infrastructure** for e-governance.
- Design applications for **e-services** (e.g., UIDAI, Aadhar, GSTN).
- Focus on **cybersecurity** and **data protection systems**.
